### Unit Economics — Ankit session (May 12) — Oneday MBA

**The core formula:** Price − COGS − CAC = Unit Profit. If negative, the model doesn't work. Full stop.

**Key frameworks:**
- Viability is designable — you're not stuck with initial numbers. Tweak CAC, price, cost.
- Most founders underestimate CAC and overestimate willingness to pay.
- Price must exceed COGS + CAC to have any margin.
- LTV:CAC ratio — target 3:1 or better. Under 1:1 = the model won't survive.

**Pathset as a marketplace:**
- Revenue = take rate × average transaction value × average transactions per user
- Onboarding/support cost per user + CAC eaten from that revenue
- If profit per user is negative: raise take rate, boost user activity, reduce onboarding friction.

**Boost margin with innovation:**
- Freemium → paid (Spotify, Dropbox model)
- Usage-based billing (Zapier, MailChimp)
- Upsells and add-ons (this is where the real money is — gas station model: make it on the add-ons, not the base transaction)

**Ankit's founding story:** Ran a gym chain in China to 20K members, helped a tech company through $200M funding round, built Flame app (relationships), featured in TechCrunch. His point: he learned more outside the classroom than inside it.
